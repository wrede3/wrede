Recommended restaurants, cafés, and bars

In the following, you find a list of restaurants, cafés, and bars that won my heart. If you are in the neighbourhood, consider checking out these places. In case you manage to go to any of these places, let me know what you think!

Aachen

Aachen has a surprisingly high density of very good Chinese restaurants! This is really convenient for me as I like Chinese food.

  • City garden: Come here for very nice all-you-can-eat Chinese Hot Pot! While I’ve been to nice Hot Pot restaurants in Düsseldorf, Munich, and Paris, this place stays my favourite. Spicy broth is a must.
  • Grotesque bar: It’s an Absinth-bar! The staff is very friendly and always prepared for a good recommendation. The location itself is also very charming, feels like someone’s livingroom. I really like their cocktail “Call of Cthulhu”, which has a creamy consistency and a slightly sweet flavor.

  • Mostard:
  • Xi’An: Delicious Chinese hand-pulled noodles. I recommend the Biangbiang-noodles, which have a nice balance of spiciness and Chinese black vinegar.

Düsseldorf

I guess, I don’t have to mention that Düsseldorf is famous for its “Little Tokyo” close to the central train station. Going there is definitively a nice experience with all the Japanese restaurants, bars, supermarkets, and everyday shops. However, if you come at the wrong time, waiting times are high. If you are not in the mood to wait so much, consider checking out one of the following places (still, they are good, just less hyped).

  • Hülsmann:
  • Korea Haus: This place is right next to “Little Tokyo”. Korea Haus offers Korean BBQ, where you have to grill your own food. It’s a nice activity with friends. However, if you are one of those people who don’t want to prepare their own food at a restaurant, fear not: You can also order food that is ready-to-eat.

Munich

  • Tushita Teehaus: This place offers a huge variety of teas, like special Pu Erh teas, white teas, Oolong teas, etc. Chinese teas are served Gongfu-style. That is, you brew a lot of tea with little water for a few seconds, and repeat this process a few times. In each brew, the tea slightly changes its flavour. It’s a great place to rest or learn about tea. I recommend the Alishan High Mountain Oolong tea.
  • Yanyou: More delicious Chinese food! I really enjoyed their Home style Tofu.
